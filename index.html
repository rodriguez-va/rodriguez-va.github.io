Victor A. Rodriguez 
    

I am a theoretical physicist working at Princeton University as an associate research scholar.

The two main subjects of my research are fundamental aspects of string theory and bootstrap techniques to solve strongly coupled physical systems.

Recently, I have been interested in using string theory to learn about the quantum dynamics of particles in cosmological spacetimes; using string theory to study non-perturbative quantum effects in two-dimensional gravity; and bootstrapping statistical systems on a lattice. 

Research interests

  • worldsheet string theory: D-instantons, 2d string theories, matrix models and duality
  • cosmological backgrounds in string theory
  • bootstrap techniques to solve strongly coupled physical systems
